## Overview

An end-to-end data analysis and predictive modelling project exploring COVID-19 trends at both global and country level, with a deep focus on Nigeria. The project covers data loading, cleaning, exploratory analysis, interactive visualisation, and polynomial regression forecasting applied to real epidemiological data spanning 2020 to 2024.

## Dataset

Loaded directly from the OWID GitHub repository — no manual download required.

---

## What This Project Covers

**Global Analysis**

- Identified the Top 10 countries by total COVID-19 cases using an interactive Plotly bar chart
- Global totals: 775,866,783 total cases and 7,057,132 total deaths worldwide
- Top 10 ranked: United States (103M), China (99M), India (45M), France (39M), Germany (38M), Brazil (37M), South Korea (34M), Japan (33M), Italy (26M), United Kingdom (24M)

**Nigeria Deep Dive**

- Total COVID-19 Cases Over Time — cumulative cases growing from near zero in 2020 to approximately 267,000 by mid 2024
- Total Deaths Over Time — cumulative deaths reaching approximately 3,155 by 2024
- Total Vaccinations Over Time — rollout beginning in early 2021, reaching approximately 133 million doses by 2024

**Predictive Modelling**

- Built a Polynomial Regression model (Degree 4) on Nigeria case data
- Forecasted the next 90 days of total case trajectory
- Plotted actual vs predicted values to validate model fit

---

## Key Insights

- The United States accounted for more total cases than China and India combined
- Nigeria maintained a very low case fatality rate relative to its total case count
- Nigeria's vaccination rollout accelerated significantly through 2022 and 2023
- The polynomial regression model captures the plateau in Nigeria's case curve after 2022
